A: The maximum amount you can sue for in Small Claims Court is $5,000.[S2]

**Q: What happens if the defendant doesn't show up?**
A: If the defendant fails to appear, the court may enter a default judgment in your favor, provided you present sufficient evidence to support your claim.[S2]

**Q: Can I appeal?**
A: Yes, either party can appeal a Small Claims Court decision by filing a Notice of Appeal within 45 days of the judgment.[S2]

**Q: How long is a judgment good for?**
A: A judgment is valid for 20 years and can be renewed for an additional 20 years if necessary.[unverified]

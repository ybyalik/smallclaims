## 1. Jurisdictional limits

- **Most you can sue for:** $10,000 for individuals; $10,000 for businesses [S3].
- **Annual filing cap:** No person can file more than 20 small claims cases in one calendar year. Kan. Stat. § 61-2703.
- **Splitting a claim:** If a claim exceeds the $10,000 cap, you must either waive the excess amount to stay in small claims court or file in a higher court. Splitting one dispute into multiple claims to fit under the cap is not allowed. [S3]
- **What's excluded from the cap:** interest and court costs are excluded [S3].

## 17. Common pitfalls and dismissal reasons

### Exceeding the claim limit

**What goes wrong:** If your claim exceeds $10,000, the court may dismiss the case or require you to waive the excess amount.

**How to avoid it:** Ensure your claim, excluding interest and costs, does not exceed the $10,000 limit. [S3]

### Improper service of process

**What goes wrong:** Failure to properly serve the defendant can result in delays or dismissal of your case.

**How to avoid it:** Follow Kansas statutes for service of process and consider using the sheriff's office or a professional process server. [S10]

### Missing the statute of limitations

**What goes wrong:** Filing after the statute of limitations has expired will result in dismissal of your case.

**How to avoid it:** Verify the applicable statute of limitations for your claim type before filing. [S6]

### Failing to collect judgment

**What goes wrong:** Winning a judgment does not guarantee payment; you may need to take additional steps to collect.

**How to avoid it:** Familiarize yourself with post-judgment collection methods such as garnishment and liens. [S12]

### Not bringing necessary evidence

**What goes wrong:** Lack of evidence can lead to losing your case, even if you're in the right.

**How to avoid it:** Bring all relevant documents, receipts, contracts, and witness information to court. [unverified]

### Ignoring counterclaims

**What goes wrong:** If the defendant files a counterclaim and you are unprepared, you could lose money.

**How to avoid it:** Be prepared to defend against potential counterclaims by gathering evidence and understanding your rights. [unverified]

A: Most small claims cases in Kansas are resolved within 30 to 90 days from filing to judgment.

**Q: What's the maximum I can sue for?**  
A: The maximum amount you can sue for in Kansas small claims court is $10,000, excluding interest and court costs. [S3]

**Q: What happens if the defendant doesn't show up?**  
A: If the defendant fails to appear, the court may grant a default judgment in favor of the plaintiff.

**Q: Can I appeal?**  
A: Yes, either party can appeal a small claims judgment within 14 days by filing a notice of appeal and paying the required fee. [S11]

**Q: How long is a judgment good for?**  
A: A judgment in Kansas is valid for 5 years and can be renewed before expiration to extend its enforceability.

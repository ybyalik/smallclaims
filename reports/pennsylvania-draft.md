## 1. Jurisdictional limits

### Contracts and money owed

Pennsylvania allows you 4 years to file a claim for breach of contract, whether written or oral.

- **Breach of a written contract** — eligible.
  - **Example:** A contractor signed a $10,000 contract to remodel your kitchen, took the deposit, and never completed the work.

- **Breach of an oral contract** — eligible.
  - **Example:** You agreed verbally to lend someone $5,000, and they failed to repay as promised.
  - **Statute:** 42 Pa.C.S. § 5525. Also a 4-year deadline. Gather evidence like emails, texts, or witness statements to prove the agreement. [S4]
- **Unpaid personal loan** — eligible.
  - **Example:** You loaned a friend $3,000 with the understanding they'd repay in 6 months, but they haven't.
  - **Statute:** 42 Pa.C.S. § 5525. File within 4 years. Documentation of the loan terms and any repayment attempts will support your case. [S4]
- **Default on a promissory note** — eligible.
  - **Example:** A borrower signed a promissory note for $8,000 and missed the repayment deadline.
  - **Statute:** 42 Pa.C.S. § 5525. You have 4 years to file. Present the original note and records of missed payments. [S4]
- **Unpaid invoice for services rendered** — eligible.
  - **Example:** You provided $2,500 worth of consulting services, but the client hasn't paid the invoice.

- **Trade-name (DBA) rule:** Businesses operating under a fictitious name must register with the state and may need to provide proof in court.; form: MDJ-14 (Fictitious Name Registration).
- **Licensing notes:** Unlicensed contractors may face challenges in enforcing contracts; consult legal counsel.

- **Allegheny** (E-filing availability) — Allegheny County offers e-filing for certain cases; check with the local court. [S1]

## 17. Common pitfalls and dismissal reasons

### Incorrect defendant information

**What goes wrong:** Suing the wrong party or using incorrect names can result in dismissal or unenforceable judgments.

**How to avoid it:** Verify the exact legal name and address of the defendant before filing. [S1]

### Missing statute of limitations

**What goes wrong:** Filing after the deadline results in automatic dismissal of the case.

**How to avoid it:** Confirm the applicable statute of limitations for your claim type before filing. [S4]

### Improper service of process

**What goes wrong:** Failure to serve the defendant correctly can delay the case or lead to dismissal.

**How to avoid it:** Follow the court's rules for service of process precisely. [S12]

### Insufficient evidence

**What goes wrong:** Lack of evidence can result in losing the case, even if your claim is valid.

**How to avoid it:** Gather all relevant documents, photos, and witness statements before the hearing. [S1]

### Ignoring counterclaims

**What goes wrong:** Defendants may file counterclaims, potentially increasing your liability.

**How to avoid it:** Be prepared to defend against possible counterclaims by reviewing your case thoroughly. [S1]

### Overlooking post-judgment procedures

**What goes wrong:** Winning a judgment doesn't guarantee payment; you must take steps to collect.

**How to avoid it:** Familiarize yourself with collection methods and follow through promptly. [S15]

## 19. FAQs

**Q: Do I need a lawyer?**  
A: No, you are not required to have a lawyer in Magisterial District Court. Many individuals represent themselves in small claims cases.

**Q: How long does it take?**  
A: Small claims cases typically resolve within 30 to 90 days, depending on the court's schedule and complexity of the case.

**Q: What's the maximum I can sue for?**  
A: The maximum amount you can sue for in Pennsylvania's small claims court is $12,000.

**Q: What happens if the defendant doesn't show up?**  
A: If the defendant fails to appear, the court may enter a default judgment in your favor.

**Q: Can I appeal?**  
A: Yes, either party can appeal the judgment to the Court of Common Pleas within 30 days.

**Q: How long is a judgment good for?**  
A: A judgment in Pennsylvania is valid for 20 years and can be renewed before expiration.

## 19. FAQs

**Q: Do I need a lawyer?**
A: No, attorneys are generally not allowed in Washington small claims court unless the judge grants permission. The process is designed for individuals to represent themselves. [S1]

**Q: How long does it take?**
A: The timeline varies, but most small claims cases are resolved within 30 to 90 days from filing to judgment.

**Q: What's the maximum I can sue for?**
A: Individuals can sue for up to $10,000, while businesses and other entities have a limit of $5,000. [S1]

**Q: What happens if the defendant doesn't show up?**
A: If the defendant fails to appear at the hearing, the court may enter a default judgment in favor of the plaintiff.

**Q: Can I appeal?**
A: Yes, either party can appeal a small claims judgment to the superior court within 30 days, provided certain conditions are met. [S14]

**Q: How long is a judgment good for?**
A: A judgment is valid for 10 years and can be renewed for an additional 10 years if necessary.

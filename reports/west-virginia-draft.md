## 1. Jurisdictional limits

- **Most you can sue for:** $10,000 for individuals; $10,000 for businesses [S4].

Seize funds from the debtor's bank account to satisfy the judgment.

Obtain a writ of execution from the court, serve it on the bank, and the bank freezes and turns over funds to satisfy the judgment.

## 17. Common pitfalls and dismissal reasons

### Incorrect defendant information

**What goes wrong:** Suing the wrong party or using incorrect names can result in dismissal or unenforceable judgments.

**How to avoid it:** Verify the exact legal name and address of the defendant before filing; for businesses, check with the Secretary of State. [S4]

### Missing statute of limitations

**What goes wrong:** Filing after the statute of limitations expires leads to automatic dismissal.

**How to avoid it:** Confirm the applicable statute of limitations for your claim type before filing. [S4]

### Improper service of process

**What goes wrong:** Failure to serve the defendant correctly can delay the case or result in dismissal.

**How to avoid it:** Follow the court's rules for service of process precisely; consider using the sheriff's office for service. [S4]

### Insufficient evidence

**What goes wrong:** Lack of evidence can lead to losing the case, even if your claim is valid.

**How to avoid it:** Gather all relevant documents, photos, and witness statements before the hearing. [S4]

### Ignoring counterclaims

**What goes wrong:** If the defendant files a counterclaim and you don't respond, you could lose by default.

**How to avoid it:** Respond to any counterclaims promptly and prepare your defense. [S4]

### Failing to collect judgment

**What goes wrong:** Winning a judgment doesn't guarantee payment; you must take steps to collect.

**How to avoid it:** Learn about collection methods like garnishment and liens; act promptly to enforce the judgment. [S4]

## 19. FAQs

**Q: Do I need a lawyer?**  
A: No, you are not required to have a lawyer in Magistrate Court; individuals and businesses can represent themselves. [S4]

**Q: How long does it take?**  
A: The timeline varies, but most small claims cases are resolved within 30 to 90 days from filing to judgment. [S4]

**Q: What's the maximum I can sue for?**  
A: You can sue for up to $10,000 in Magistrate Court; claims exceeding this amount must be filed in Circuit Court. [S4]

**Q: What happens if the defendant doesn't show up?**  
A: If the defendant fails to appear, the court may grant a default judgment in your favor. [S4]

**Q: Can I appeal?**  
A: Yes, either party can appeal a Magistrate Court decision to the Circuit Court within 20 days for a trial de novo. [S7]

**Q: How long is a judgment good for?**  
A: A judgment is valid for 10 years and can be renewed before it expires. [S8]
